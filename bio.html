Childhood Years

            On April 5, 2006, I was born in Quezon City, and my family had a tremendous influence on my early years. 1 From the moment I could walk and talk, my world was filled with imagination, adventure, and excitement. 2 My parents encouraged me to explore and learn, always supporting my curiosity. 3 Every day, I was constantly discovering something new, whether it was through my parents' teachings, playing outside with friends, or simply observing the world around me. 4 My sisters, being older and more experienced, often guided me through childhood challenges and shaped my interests. 5 I have three older sisters, and they played a huge role in influencing my hobbies, choices, and even the way I saw the world. 6 Whether it was playing games, watching movies, or talking about life, they were always there. 7 Going to school was another adventure in itself. I enjoyed meeting new people, making friends, and learning about different subjects that broadened my understanding of the world. 8 I was particularly drawn to creative activities, technology, and anything that allowed me to express myself. 9 It was during this time that I developed a passion for computers and technology, all thanks to my introduction to gaming. 10 I remember the excitement I felt when I first played a video game, completely immersed in a world beyond reality. 11 That moment sparked my love for technology, and it has continued to grow ever since. 12 Despite being the youngest, I never felt left out. 13 My family made sure I was loved, supported, and taken care of, which made me feel like the main character in a movie. 14 There was never a dull moment in our home, as laughter, love, and a sense of belonging filled every corner. 15 Looking back, I don’t remember every single detail of my childhood, but I do remember the warmth and happiness that surrounded me. 16 The memories of running around our neighborhood, playing with my sisters, and celebrating birthdays remain vivid in my heart. 17 Even the small things—like watching cartoons on Saturday mornings or eating my favorite homemade meals—hold a special place in my mind. As I reflect on my early years, I realize how lucky and grateful I am to have experienced such a wonderful childhood. 18Those moments shaped the person I am today, giving me a strong foundation of love, curiosity, and resilience. 19 My family’s unwavering support gave me the confidence to explore new things and follow my passions. 20 Without them, I wouldn’t have developed the interests and values that guide me now. 21 Though childhood eventually faded into teenage years, the lessons and love I received during that time will always stay with me. 22

Teenage Years

            During my teenage years, life felt like a crammed rollercoaster of emotions, experiences, and lessons. 1 Like many teenagers, the first two years of high school were marked by the typical rebellious phase. There were moments when I fought with my parents over rules, craved more freedom, and prioritized my friends’ opinions over family guidance. 2 At the time, I didn’t realize how much my actions and attitude would eventually haunt me. 3 This continued until the end of 9th grade, when the COVID-19 pandemic began—turning my whole world upside down. 4 The last memory I shared with my father was him saying goodbye before leaving for a checkup. 5 At the time, I didn’t think much of it; I figured he was just going in for a routine visit and would get better. 6 I was so caught up in my own world that I didn’t even bother to hug him goodbye. 8 Instead, I stayed glued to my screen, playing video games with my friends, unaware that this would be the last time I would ever see him alive. 9 Weeks passed, and I believed he was recovering, but one night, my mom woke me and my sisters up, sobbing while on the phone. 10 Through her tears, she told us that Papa was gone and that we needed to go to the hospital. 11 In that moment, my entire world shattered, and I felt a pain I had never known before. 12 The guilt weighed heavily on me, and I found myself trapped in an endless cycle of regret. 13 I started gaining a lot of weight, neglecting my health, and isolating myself from everyone around me. 14 I felt like my world had completely ended, and I didn’t know how to move forward. 15 My mind was filled with "what-ifs" and "what could have been," tormenting me every single day. 16 I eventually fell into depression, struggling to find meaning in anything. 17 Even now, I still dream about the moments I wish I could redo, the words I wish I had said, and the time I wish I could have spent with my father. 18 The loss changed me in ways I never expected, forcing me to see life from a different perspective. 19 Though the pain never truly goes away, I have learned to carry it with me and use it as a reminder to cherish the people I love while they are still here. 20

College Years

            College was a turning point in my life. 1 It was the stage where I truly stepped into independence, balancing academics, social life, and personal growth. 2 I’m currently pursuing my studies in BSIT at the University of the East Manila. 3 Growing up in a techsavvy family, I always had a natural curiosity for computers and digital systems. 4 I would say that I kind of always knew BSIT was the course I would take, but actually studying it made me appreciate it even more. 5 The journey, however, hasn’t always been easy—long nights of studying, tight deadlines, and the challenge of adapting to a new environment constantly test my resilience. 6 Despite the challenges, college has also been a time of incredible experiences. 7 I have met people who have inspired me, supported me, and made this journey worthwhile. 8 I made lifelong friends who share the same struggles, ambitions, and late-night study sessions. 9 Socially, college has helped me come out of my shell and build more confidence. 10 I used to be shy when meeting new people, but being in college has pushed me to socialize, connect, and create valuable relationships. 11 There were times when I doubted myself, questioning if I had chosen the right path, but every small achievement reminded me why I was here. 12 One of my proudest moments in college was the first time I got a high mark on an exam. 13 That moment made me realize that this was the path I wanted to take because, for the first time, I actually enjoyed reviewing. 14 Learning became something I looked forward to rather than something I just had to do. 15 I still have two years before I graduate, and I am so excited for what’s to come. 16 The idea of stepping into the real world, applying what I’ve learned, and building a career in IT motivates me every single day. 17 While the future is uncertain, I know that as long as I stay determined, I will achieve my goals. 18 College has been a journey of self-discovery, challenges, and triumphs, and I wouldn’t trade this experience for anything. 19
